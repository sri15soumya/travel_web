Explore Goa's Rich Culture & Heritage

Goa offers stunning beaches, vibrant festivals, affordable alcohol, delicious seafood cuisine, lively nightlife, and unique shopping experiences. Visitors can enjoy picturesque sunsets, relax on soft sandy shores, and choose from bustling beaches in North Goa.

Temples

Mangueshi Temple

Shri Manguesh temple (Devanagari: श्री मंगेश मंदिर) is a Hindu temple located at Mangeshi Village in Priol, Ponda taluk, Goa. It is at a distance of 1 km from Mardol close to Nagueshi, 21 km from Panaji, the capital of Goa, and 26 km from Margao. Shree Mangueshi is the Kuldeva (family deity) of Saraswat Brahmins and other gotras. Shrimad Swamiji of Shri Kavale Math is the Spiritual Chief of Shri Manguesh Saunsthan, Mangueshi. This temple is one of the largest and most frequently visited temples in Goa.

Cost: ₹100 per person

Duration: 1-2 hours

Mahadev Temple

Mahadeva Temple, Tambdi Surla is a 13th-century Hindu temple in the Kadamba style dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the main deities in Hinduism, and is an active place of Hindu worship. It is an ASI-protected Monument of National Importance in Goa. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is reminiscent of the temples at Aihole in neighbouring Karnataka.

Cost: ₹150 per person

Duration: 1-2 hours

Churches

Se Cathedral

The word Sé is Portuguese for See. The Se Cathedral was built to commemorate the victory of the Portuguese under Afonso de Albuquerque over a Muslim army, leading to the capture of the city of Goa in 1510. Since the day of the victory happened to be on the feast of Saint Catherine, the cathedral was dedicated to her. It was commissioned by Governor George Cabral to be enlarged in 1552 on the remains of an earlier structure.

Cost: ₹200 per person

Duration: 1-2 hours

Monuments

Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous churches in Goa. It houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier and is renowned for its Baroque architecture. The basilica, constructed in 1605, is a prime example of Jesuit architecture and is one of the oldest churches in India. The interior is adorned with intricate carvings and gilded altars, making it a must-visit for history and architecture enthusiasts.

Cost: ₹100 per person

Duration: 1-2 hours

Aguada Fort

Aguada Fort is a well-preserved 17th-century Portuguese fort overlooking the Arabian Sea. It was built to guard against Dutch and Maratha invaders and features a lighthouse and a freshwater spring. The fort's strategic location offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and the sea. The lighthouse, one of the oldest in Asia, is a prominent landmark and provides a glimpse into Goa's maritime history. The fort's massive walls and bastions are a testament to the architectural prowess of the Portuguese.

Cost: ₹150 per person

Duration: 1-2 hours

Beaches

Calangute Beach

Calangute is the most popular beach with thousands thronging it in both the peak and off-season. The waves rise high above as you wash away your city blues, though swimmers need to be cautious because of the sudden drop and the rising waves. Experienced swimmers will, however, revel in the seas here. The beach is fringed with popular restaurants and hotels, including the Calangute Residency operated by GTDC. This long, seven-km sweep of sand located 15 km from Panaji is called the 'Queen of Beaches'.

Cost: Free entry

Duration: Flexible

Baga Beach

Baga Beach is a popular beach and tourist destination in North Goa. Baga is located at the north end of the contiguous beach stretch that starts from Sinquerim, Candolim, leads to Calangute, and then to Baga. Dolphins can be located at around 1–2 km from the shoreline. The beach contains rows of shacks and fishing boats, and at high tide, the beach is narrow. The beach is named after the Baga Creek, which empties into the Arabian Sea at the north end of the beach.

Cost: Free entry

Duration: Flexible

Adventure

White Water Rafting

Known for its serene beaches and vibrant nightlife, Goa also offers exciting river adventures amidst its lush landscapes. The Mhadei River, one of the most popular spots for white water rafting in Goa, offers challenging rapids surrounded by the dense forests of the Western Ghats. This natural setting makes rafting here a perfect blend of excitement and scenic beauty. The journey through the rapids can be exhilarating, as the strong currents take you through twists and turns, providing a unique perspective on Goa's lesser-known natural wonders.

Cost: ₹1500 per person

Duration: 2-3 hours

Paragliding

Everybody loves Arambol beach as it is a sight offering the best of both worlds. Not only does it offer peaceful solitude but also an enjoyable nightlife. It is located in North Goa, a favored tourist zone among all. Here you will witness a lot of people enjoying adventurous sports, with paragliding being the most enjoyable. The flight here starts from a cliff located between Harmal and Arambol beaches. After taking off, you will be mesmerized to witness the view of plains and mountains that accompanies the long shoreline of the pristine beach.

Cost: ₹3000 per person

Duration: 1 hour

    Travel Safety Guide

    Stay safe during your travels with these essential safety tips.

    1. Keep Your Documents Safe

    Make digital copies of your passport, visa, and important documents.

    2. Stay Aware of Your Surroundings

    Avoid unsafe areas and be cautious of common travel scams.

    3. Emergency Contacts

    Always have local police, hospital, and embassy contact numbers.

    4. Health & Hygiene Safety

    Drink bottled water, wash hands regularly, and be cautious of street food. Carry essential medications.

    5. Stay Connected

    Share your itinerary with family/friends and have a local SIM card or roaming plan for emergencies.

    6. Natural Disaster Preparedness

    Check weather conditions before traveling. Know emergency evacuation procedures in your destination.

    Weather & Seasonal Safety

    Knowing the weather of your destination helps you pack the right clothes and avoid extreme conditions.

    • Summer (June - August): High temperatures in tropical and desert regions. Stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and avoid direct sun exposure.
    • Monsoon (July - September): Heavy rains in Southeast Asia and tropical regions. Be prepared for flight delays and possible flooding.
    • Winter (December - February): Cold temperatures in Europe and North America. Wear layered clothing, check road conditions, and watch out for frostbite.
    • Spring & Fall: Ideal for travel in most regions. Pack light layers for temperature fluctuations.

    Emergency Contacts

    • Police: 112 / 911
    • Medical Assistance: 999 / Local Hospitals
    • Fire Department: 101 / 911
    • Embassy Assistance: Find your embassy

    Additional Travel Safety Advice

    ✔ Always lock your hotel room and avoid displaying valuables in public.

    ✔ Learn basic phrases in the local language to communicate in emergencies.

    ✔ Register your trip with your country's embassy for alerts and safety updates.

    ✔ Avoid political gatherings or protests, as they can quickly become unsafe.
