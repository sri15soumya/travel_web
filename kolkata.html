Explore Kolkata – The City of Joy!

Kolkata, the cultural capital of India, is a city that beautifully blends colonial charm with modern vibrancy. Known for its rich history, artistic heritage, and literary legacy, Kolkata is home to stunning monuments, grand temples, and thrilling adventure activities. Whether you're a history enthusiast, a spiritual seeker, or an adrenaline junkie, Kolkata has something special for you.

Historic Monuments

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is one of Kolkata’s most iconic landmarks, built in memory of Queen Victoria. This grand marble structure is surrounded by lush gardens and houses an extensive museum showcasing the colonial history of India. The light and sound show held in the evenings is a must-watch experience.

Cost: ₹30 (Indian nationals), ₹500 (Foreign tourists)

Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM (Closed on Mondays)

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge, an engineering marvel, is one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world. Spanning the Hooghly River, this iconic structure connects Kolkata to Howrah and is a lifeline for the city's daily commuters. The view of the bridge at sunrise or sunset is simply breathtaking.

Cost: Free

Timings: Open 24 hours

Marble Palace

The Marble Palace is a 19th-century mansion famous for its stunning marble architecture and an extensive collection of artworks, including Western sculptures, paintings, and antique furniture. It is a hidden gem in the city for art and history lovers.

Cost: Free (Special permit required)

Timings: 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM (Closed on Mondays and Thursdays)

Temples

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

One of the most revered temples in Kolkata, Dakshineswar Kali Temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali. Located on the banks of the Hooghly River, the temple is famous for its beautiful architecture and spiritual ambiance.

Entry Fee: Free

Timings: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM

Belur Math

Founded by Swami Vivekananda, Belur Math is a serene place that promotes interfaith harmony. The temple’s unique architecture blends Hindu, Islamic, and Christian elements, symbolizing unity among religions.

Entry Fee: Free

Timings: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM

ISKCON Kolkata

The ISKCON Temple in Kolkata is a place of devotion and spirituality dedicated to Lord Krishna. It offers a peaceful retreat with devotional chants, lectures, and delicious vegetarian meals.

Entry Fee: Free

Timings: 4:30 AM – 9:00 PM

Adventure in Kolkata

Sundarbans Safari

Embark on an unforgettable adventure in the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest. Home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger, this UNESCO World Heritage Site offers thrilling boat safaris through its dense waterways.

Cost: ₹3000 per person (approx.)

Timings: Depends on safari schedule

Eco Park Adventure

Eco Park offers a variety of adventure activities, including kayaking, zorbing, and cycling. It is a perfect place for thrill-seekers looking for a mix of nature and excitement.

Cost: ₹30 (entry), additional charges for activities

Timings: 12:00 PM – 7:30 PM

    Weather & Seasonal Safety

    Knowing Kolkata’s weather conditions helps you plan your trip and pack accordingly for a comfortable experience.

    • Summer (April - June): Hot and humid, with temperatures ranging from 35°C to 40°C (95°F to 104°F). High humidity levels can make it feel even hotter. Stay hydrated, wear breathable cotton clothing, and avoid outdoor activities during peak afternoon hours.
    • Monsoon (July - September): Heavy rainfall with frequent thunderstorms, leading to waterlogging and potential travel delays. Carry an umbrella or raincoat, and check the weather forecast before heading out.
    • Winter (December - February): Cool and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F). Mornings and evenings can be chilly, so light woolens or a jacket may be needed.
