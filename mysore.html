Explore Mysore – The Cultural Capital of Karnataka!

Mysore, known as the City of Palaces, is a treasure trove of history, spirituality, and adventure. The city boasts magnificent monuments, sacred temples, and thrilling activities that attract visitors from all over the world. Whether you're an explorer, a devotee, or an adventure seeker, Mysore has something truly special to offer.

Historic Monuments

Mysore Palace

The Mysore Palace is an architectural masterpiece and one of the most visited landmarks in India. Its Indo-Saracenic style, intricate interiors, and grand halls reflect the city's royal heritage. The palace is illuminated with over 97,000 bulbs during special occasions, making it a mesmerizing sight at night.

Cost: ₹70 (Indian nationals), ₹200 (Foreign tourists)

Timings: 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Jaganmohan Palace

Once a royal residence, Jaganmohan Palace now serves as an art gallery showcasing an extensive collection of paintings and artifacts. The palace is famous for housing the works of Raja Ravi Varma and other renowned artists.

Cost: ₹20 per person

Timings: 8:30 AM – 5:30 PM

Lalitha Mahal

Built in 1921, Lalitha Mahal is a striking white palace inspired by St. Paul’s Cathedral in London. Now a luxury hotel, it retains its royal charm with exquisite chandeliers, marble floors, and sprawling gardens.

Cost: Free (Hotel guests), Entry fee for visitors

Timings: 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Famous Temples

Chamundeshwari Temple

Perched atop Chamundi Hills, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari, the presiding deity of Mysore. The temple's intricate carvings, towering gopuram, and divine aura make it a revered pilgrimage site.

Cost: Free

Timings: 7:30 AM – 9:00 PM

Trinesvaraswamy Temple

Located near Mysore Palace, this ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is known for its serene ambiance and the intricate Dravidian-style architecture.

Cost: Free

Timings: 6:00 AM – 12:00 PM, 4:00 PM – 8:00 PM

Nanjangud Temple

Situated on the banks of the Kabini River, this temple is dedicated to Lord Srikanteshwara, a form of Shiva. It is often called the "Varanasi of the South" and is one of the largest temples in Karnataka.

Cost: Free

Timings: 6:00 AM – 8:30 PM

Zip-lining at Chamundi Hills

Experience the thrill of zip-lining over the lush green valleys of Chamundi Hills. A perfect adventure for adrenaline seekers.

Cost: ₹1500 per person

Timings: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Enjoy panoramic views of Mysore from the sky. The experience is magical, especially at sunrise when the city is bathed in golden light.

Cost: ₹8000 per person

Timings: 5:30 AM – 7:00 AM

    Weather & Seasonal Safety

    Knowing the weather in Mysore helps you pack appropriately and plan your travel with comfort in mind.

    • Summer (March - June): Warm and dry, with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 35°C (77°F to 95°F). Stay hydrated, wear light cotton clothing, and avoid outdoor activities during peak afternoon hours.
    • Monsoon (July - September): Moderate to heavy rainfall, bringing lush greenery and cooler temperatures. Carry an umbrella or raincoat, and be prepared for occasional travel delays due to rain.
    • Winter (October - February): Pleasant and cool, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C (59°F to 77°F). Ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Light woolens may be needed for early mornings and late evenings.
